# PayLedger AI — 3-Minute Demo Script

A scene-by-scene walkthrough for the OPay Scholars National Innovation Challenge
demo. Timings are a guide — the whole run fits comfortably in **3 minutes**.

> **Prototype only. PayLedger AI does not process or verify real payments.**
> All figures are mock data for demonstration.

**Setup:** run `npm install && npm run dev`, open the printed URL, and use a
**phone-width window** (or your browser's device toolbar at ~390px). Start on the
landing page.

---

## 0:00 – 0:30 · The problem (landing page)

> "Millions of Nigerian campus vendors, student entrepreneurs, POS agents and
> informal traders take money every day — cash, transfer, POS, QR. But the
> **record disappears** the moment the payment lands. No trusted history means
> disputes, forgotten debts, and no path to credit."

- Point to the headline: **"Every payment becomes a trusted record."**
- One line of positioning: *"This is not an expense tracker. It's business
  memory, fraud awareness, and credit-readiness for informal commerce."*

**Tap “Open merchant dashboard.”**

---

## 0:30 – 1:15 · The dashboard (the 10-second story)

> "This is Amaka's Campus Kitchen — a real-feeling merchant workspace."

- **Banner**: greeting + **credit readiness 76/100** front and centre.
- **Quick actions**: Record sale · New debt · Scan QR · View report — "the
  everyday things a merchant does after a payment."
- **Today at a glance**: Sales ₦82,500 · Profit ₦21,800 · Outstanding debt
  ₦13,500 · Stock alerts 4 · Credit readiness 76/100 · Risk alerts 2.
- **AI Insight card** (read it aloud — this is the Gemini-style coach):
  > "Your food sales increased today. Rice and drinks sold fastest. Restock
  > drinks before evening rush and follow up with customers owing ₦13,500."
- **Recent transactions**: note the four payment types — **Cash, Transfer, POS,
  QR** — and the one flagged "Needs review."

> "In ten seconds a judge sees sales, profit, debt, risk, an AI recommendation,
> and mixed payment methods — that's the whole product story."

---

## 1:15 – 1:40 · Smart Ledger

**Tap “Ledger.”**

> "Every payment becomes a trusted record. Filter by method —"

- Tap **Transfer**, then **POS** — the list and the running total update.
- Banner highlight shows **today's sales** at a glance.

> "Business memory, not bookkeeping — who paid, how, and how much."

---

## 1:40 – 2:05 · Debt Book + Fraud Shield

**Tap “Debt.”**

> "Track who's buying on credit. ₦13,500 outstanding across three customers,
> each with a risk level so Amaka follows up before small debts go bad."

**Tap “Fraud.”**

> "Fraud-risk **guidance** — not bank verification. A suspicious-payment log
> flags an unconfirmed transfer, plus a fraud-awareness checklist."

- Tick a checklist item to show it's interactive.

---

## 2:05 – 2:40 · Credit Readiness

**Tap “Report.”**

> "Clean records produce a credit-readiness **report** — not a loan approval."

- Point to the **76/100 gauge** and the factors that shape it (consistent
  records, mixed payments, debt recovery, fraud hygiene).
- Scroll to the **inventory tracker** — drinks critical, restock before the
  evening rush (ties back to the AI insight).

---

## 2:40 – 3:00 · Why it fits the challenge

Return to the landing page and scroll to **Innovation fit**:

> "OPay — supports merchants *after* the payment. Google/Gemini — AI insight and
> coaching. 3MTT — software, AI, data analysis, UI/UX and cybersecurity
> awareness. And it starts with students and campus vendors, then scales to the
> wider informal economy."

**Close:**

> "PayLedger AI — AI business memory for campus and informal commerce. A
> prototype today; it does not process or verify real payments."

---

## One-line elevator pitch

> **PayLedger AI turns a merchant's daily cash, transfer, POS and QR payments
> into trusted records, fraud-risk guidance, debt tracking and a credit-readiness
> report — business memory for informal commerce, not an expense tracker.**

## Demo do's and don'ts

- ✅ Say "fraud-risk **guidance**," "credit-readiness **report**," "business
  memory," "after the payment."
- ❌ Don't say "verifies bank transfers," "approves loans," "detects fraud from
  bank data," or "processes payments." It does none of these — and the on-screen
  disclaimer says so.
